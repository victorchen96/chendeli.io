Abstract

The rapid advancement of foundation models has catalyzed a paradigm shift from human-AI interaction toward autonomous AI research agents. This survey provides a comprehensive analysis of systems that can independently formulate hypotheses, design experiments, execute research workflows, and synthesize findings. We propose a five-level autonomy taxonomy (L1–L5), identify four dominant architectural patterns, and systematically compare 17 major systems across a six-dimensional feature matrix. We conclude by identifying six fundamental open problems that must be addressed for the field to progress from assisted tooling to genuine research colleagues.

L1–L5 Autonomy Taxonomy

A five-level classification from simple tool augmentation (L1) through fully autonomous scientific discovery (L5), with clear boundary criteria for each level.

Architectural Patterns

Four dominant patterns identified: Pipeline, Agent-Loop, Multi-Agent, and Neuro-Symbolic — with comparative strengths analysis.

17-System Comparison

Systematic evaluation across planning depth, tool integration, verification mechanisms, domain breadth, human oversight, and output quality.

Open Problems

Six fundamental challenges: verification at scale, credit assignment, safety boundaries, multi-modal reasoning, evaluation metrics, and societal impact.
